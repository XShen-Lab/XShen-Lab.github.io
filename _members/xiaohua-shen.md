Xiaohua Shen is a tenured professor at the School of Basic Medicine, Tsinghua University, and a senior investigator of the Tsinghua-Peking Center for Life Sciences. Her research focuses on the noncoding genome, nuclear RNA, transcription, chromatin organization, nuclear architecture, and cell fate determination.

## Research

The Shen Lab studies the fundamental principles of the noncoding genome, transcription, chromatin folding, nuclear architecture, and cell fate determination. The lab aims to understand how genetic information is interpreted in the three-dimensional nuclear environment and how regulatory information flows from DNA to RNA, proteins, and cellular phenotypes.

A central goal of the lab is to uncover the physical, mathematical, and molecular logic underlying genome function, nuclear organization, and cell fate control.

## Scientific Contributions

The lab proposed that transposable repeats, including L1 and B1/Alu elements, provide a genetic and molecular basis for large-scale three-dimensional chromatin organization.

The lab also revealed that cis-regulation of neighboring genes by noncoding RNAs is a widespread regulatory mode, and that RNA-binding proteins and phase separation can feed back on transcription and chromatin states.

More broadly, the lab studies how nuclear RNA systems coordinate chromatin folding, transcription, translation, and cellular function.
