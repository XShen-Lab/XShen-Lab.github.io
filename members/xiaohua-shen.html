Xiaohua Shen is a Professor at Tsinghua University and is affiliated with the School of Basic Medicine, Center for Life Sciences, State Key Laboratory of Membrane Biology, and Beijing Frontier Research Center for Biological Structure.

Profile

Her research asks how stochastic genome activity is organized and transformed into stable biological information and diverse cellular identities. The laboratory’s framework, Biological Information Flow: From Genome to Cell Fate, connects genome organization, the RNA–RBP meshwork, transcriptional surveillance, and information dynamics with cell-fate determination.

Selected Scientific Contributions

Genome organization and spatial encoding

The laboratory showed that repetitive elements encode sequence-based principles of genome organization, with homotypic L1 and B1/Alu clustering contributing to chromatin compartmentalization and three-dimensional genome organization.

RNA meshwork and nuclear information-processing environments

Her group established genomic proximity as a principle of lncRNA-mediated cis regulation and demonstrated that nascent RNA and RNA-binding proteins help organize transcriptional machinery, nuclear environments, and cell-fate control through chromatin association, phase separation, and RNA homeostasis.

Transcriptional surveillance

The laboratory investigates how RNA Polymerase II activity, RNA processing, RNA quality control, and polymerase recycling select productive outputs from pervasive genome activity. These studies are ongoing; unpublished manuscript titles are deliberately omitted.

Probabilistic genome activity and cell-fate stabilization

The laboratory developed scFLUENT-seq to directly measure single-cell nascent transcription. The method revealed that cells use a sparse, cell-type-dependent fraction of approximately 0.02%–3.1% of the genome at a given moment, supporting a statistical view of how stable cell states emerge from stochastic transcription.

Foundations in stress signaling and epigenetic cell-fate regulation

During her graduate and postdoctoral research, Shen helped establish the XBP-1/IRE-1 unfolded-protein-response mechanism in C. elegans and identified EZH1 and JARID2 as regulators of Polycomb function, linking stress signaling and chromatin regulation to cell-fate control.
