## About Us

The XShen Lab is based in the School of Basic Medicine, Tsinghua University. Our research focuses on the noncoding genome, nuclear RNA, transcription, chromatin folding, nuclear architecture, and cell fate regulation.

We aim to understand how genetic information is interpreted, organized, and regulated in the three-dimensional nuclear environment. Our work combines molecular cell biology, functional genomics, single-cell and multi-omics approaches, imaging, computational analysis, and physical modeling.

## Research Focus

- **Noncoding genome and repetitive elements**  
  We study how noncoding genomic sequences and repetitive elements contribute to genome organization, gene regulation, and cell identity.

- **Nuclear RNA homeostasis**  
  We investigate how nuclear RNA coordinates chromatin folding, transcriptional regulation, and cellular function.

- **Chromatin folding and nuclear architecture**  
  We explore how three-dimensional genome organization emerges and how it influences gene expression programs.

- **Transcriptional regulation and phase separation**  
  We examine how transcription, RNA-binding proteins, and biomolecular condensates shape chromatin states and regulatory output.

- **Cell fate determination**  
  We seek to understand how genome regulation programs cellular identity, differentiation, and vitality.
